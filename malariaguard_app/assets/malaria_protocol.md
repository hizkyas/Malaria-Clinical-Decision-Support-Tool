# PROTOCOL: ETHIOPIAN NATIONAL MALARIA GUIDELINES (2022)

## METADATA
- **Region:** Ethiopia
- **Version:** 6th Edition (2022)
- **Target User:** Health Extension Workers (HEWs)
- **Primary Drug:** Artemether-Lumefantrine (AL / Coartem)

---

## SECTION 1: DOSAGE TABLES (ARTEMETHER-LUMEFANTRINE)
> [!IMPORTANT]
> Dosage is 20mg Artemether / 120mg Lumefantrine tablets. 
> Total of 6 doses over 3 days (Twice Daily).

| Patient Weight (kg) | Age Range (Approx) | Tablets per Dose | Total Tablets |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **5kg to <15kg** | 5 months to 2 years | 1 tablet | 6 |
| **15kg to <25kg** | 3 years to 7 years | 2 tablets | 12 |
| **25kg to <35kg** | 8 years to 13 years | 3 tablets | 18 |
| **≥35kg** | ≥14 years | 4 tablets | 24 |

---

## SECTION 2: CLINICAL DECISION LOGIC
1. **Initial Screen:** If patient has fever (≥37.5°C) or history of fever in last 48 hours.
2. **Test:** Perform Multi-species Rapid Diagnostic Test (RDT).
3. **Analyze Results:**
   - **RDT Positive (P. falciparum or P. vivax):** Proceed to Treatment Section.
   - **RDT Negative:** Search for other causes of fever (Pneumonia, Meningitis).
   - **Danger Signs Present:** DO NOT TREAT. Refer to hospital immediately.

---

## SECTION 3: SEVERE MALARIA (DANGER SIGNS)
If ANY sign is present, classify as **Severe Malaria** and **REFER IMMEDIATELY**:
- **Inability to drink** or breastfeed.
- **Persistent vomiting** (cannot keep anything down).
- **Convulsions** (fits/seizures).
- **Lethargy** or unconsciousness.
- **Inability to stand** or sit without support.
